    Turn 3 (1986)

    Trouble is brewing, especially with the reinvigoration of Soviet Russia. In your channels with LRU, you’re hearing of them getting double their funding to continue hunting down those beasts. The massive creatures are guarding treasures beneath their nests that can empower entire nations. The Templars are holding fast against the supernatural assault, but they are requesting some of the new weaponry you have developed. The few Chemical Throwers you’ve sent their way have apparently been a godsend, and the Alpha Strikers have piqued their interest.

    CSG has been doing well for themselves, with you supplying weaponry and Steinberg getting air support capability they’ve identified a few smuggling rings heading towards Europe and smashed them apart in the nick of time. Apparently Major Steinberg has reported that he is incapable of housing any more soldiers for the time being, and so the Veterans will be going to you this year.

    Your progress is pleasing, especially in terms of development of weaponry. Though your combat was barely passable in the review (Ha!) they’ve decided to once more equally halve the budget between you and your counterpart. Federal Income 250, Units Bought this turn start at Veteran (+2 to All Stats)

    You’re making nearly quadruple what they’re giving you in trade and in patents. In all honesty you could care less about their money.

    Still, the situation in China is worrying... it makes you feel powerless to be honest, sending anyone there would be practically be suicide.

    605 Income Available.

    You’ll have to make sure it never happens again.

    Learning: It’s odd to think that Rachel Timothy is now the age when you had met her. At 21, she still seems childlike, and if anything her drive to progress technology has not faltered in the slightest. No small amount of thanks towards you comes from her, for allowing her to shape the future in both warfare and in normal society. (Choose 2)

    [X] Communications: Radios are too bulky, especially the kind that will allow you to converse with a squad half-way across the country. Mobile Phone Companies have been set up in Japan and in several Nordic Countries in Europe. However, not having a call center can be rectified by using a specialized computer… Ms. Timothy insists on creating a transmitter based for computers, similar to radio, but only receivable by computers. You simply have to have them in sets and… The science behind it is new, but with your computational devices you’re sure you can figure it out quickly! Cost: 100 Upkeep: 50 Chance of Success 80% Time: 1 Year. Reward: Can Order Squads from Base, Squads Coordinate Easier. Minimum +100 Research Income on completion.

    Roll: 28 +10 = 38: Required: 20: Success

    This very nearly fails, probably because of the timetable you’ve set, but seeing its importance made you want it eagerly. The machine is reliable, but takes a bigger investment than you previously invested for that fact. However, besides the small increase in cost of the devices, you’ve created a series of transmitters that effectively allow you to stay in communication with your troops wherever they might be in the world. Most of the cost is actually the need for you to keep spreading your reliable transmitters wherever you might need them.

    Looking over it, the government is hesitant to invest in it mainly for the large cost, but you find many, many buyers among the rich and wealthy. The cordless phone systems are going to be inaccessible for the lower and middle class, where most of a profit is made, but that merely gives you an excuse to research more in this direction, and subsequently make a better, cheaper, and more reliable product for the masses.

    Rewards: +100 Research Income, Improved Communications (Cannot be listened on) Can give orders to troops from HQ. Rolls to develop. +5 Upkeep (55 Total).

    [X] Improved Body Armor: Given the fact that Rune Armor is proving to simply be better than any modern armor available, the government wants you in on the development of the new generation of body armor. It’s no small secret that all your innovations are generally being adopted by the US military, but this monumental program is the first sign they’re truly not considering you lightly. Cost: 150. Upkeep:0 (Federal Program, Won’t Require any money for you to Upkeep). Time: 2 Years. Chance of Success 90% Reward: Enhanced Infantry Armor (Adds to Squad Cost), Improved Defense (Minimum 1, Maximum 3) +150 Research Income on completion

    Roll: 82 +10 = 92

    This project goes ridiculously well, you and Ms. Timothy practically send others vying for the opportunity running home. Your proposal for a modular armor based on a long-coat design blows everything else out of the water. Whatever new materials that might be developed can simply be fitted into slots, the material composition is weatherproof and light, yet it provides better protection against explosives, overwhelming force, and attacks using bladed or pointed weapons. It’s even protective against biting attacks, and can be completely sealed around the legs with proper modifications.

    The armor is easier to produce as well, given that you and Miss Timothy utilize the computer to create a thread mesh that reduces the amount of required Kevlar yet still provides the protection of your next best competitor, if not more.

    The fact that you’ve come up with multiple attachments to quickly allow for your body armor to be changed according to the environment and current warzone is merely a bonus.

    If there was a way to speed up the retooling of factories, you’ll already have your men clad in them… along with just about every other soldier in the United States Infantry.

    Rewards: +3 Defense to Base Units (No need to pay Extra), +200 Extra Research Income, Finishes next year.

    Personal: You feel… more calm than you’ve ever been your entire life. Despite the fact the state of the world is rapidly deteriorating, you can honestly say that you sleep well at night knowing you’ve done the extent of what you can do every day. Given the reports from your fellow organization heads, you feel pained to know you’re the only one not stressed. (Choose 2)

    [X] Master Glyphs and Greater Circle Magic: You’re undoubtedly the most powerful, human magic-user in North America, perhaps even the world. The fact that you don’t know anything but a few tricks… is disappointing. Considering what you’re fighting against, learning how to use magic and learning on how to apply some of the more esoteric Runes would be a good boon for you. Cost: 0 Upkeep:0 Time 1 Year. Chance of Success: 100% Reward: Trait: Magical Arsenal: 1/2 of Piety added onto Martial. Score 50 or Greater: gain Great Coat of Greater Protection Against Miscellaneous Things (Protects against all environmental effects and defense against Monsters of Normal Strength.)

    Roll: 100: Critical Roll: 93

    You astound yourself, and perhaps even scare yourself a little bit.

    The amount of power you have at your disposal… is simply terrifying, especially when you take into account the fact you seem to be capable of utilizing more Circles at any time than anyone else on the base. To put it into perspective Barth Allen can call forth about 5 low-ranked circles, 3 mid-ranked, and 1 high-ranked circle at any given time. A high ranked circle’s firepower is just shy of that of a missile, depending mostly on what element is used as output.

    You can call forth four times his number of circles, and quickly reform them to fire again and again and again…

    Theoretically, it would take four Artillery Mage squads to keep up with your firepower, and even then they can’t last nearly as long as you can. What effectively limits you in regards to your firepower, is that you don’t have an Ultra-Ranked Circle constructed to make the full use of your abilities.

    Then, you begin to construct your means of protection in the event of battle and you blow past anything Barnes Walker ever managed. You get everything you aimed for in regards to protection, but there is just something about it that… meshes perfectly. The Great Coat of Greater Protection Against Miscellaneous Things withstood the firepower of an Alpha Strike without a single thread out of place. Even radiation doesn’t pass through it, and you weren’t even trying to get that particular protection!

    Then, to your surprise, after exhausting yourself magically day after day of practicing and crafting a magical coat that’ll probably survive a low-yield nuclear blast… you find yourself stronger.

    Rewards:

    Trait: Greater Magical Arsenal: 2/3’s of Piety Added onto Martial Score.

    +2 Piety

    Great Coat of Greater Protection Against Miscellaneous Things: Protects against all environmental effects, can only be damaged by monsters of High strength, and grants 1 re-roll in combat situations.

    [X] Getting to Know Them: Your counterparts are: Alexandra Daryna, Christopher Saint and Roderick Steinberg. Telling them of your plans certainly won’t hurt, making sure they’re not going to off themselves from stress is what you’re truly aiming for. Cost:10 Upkeep:0 Time 1 Year. Chance of Success: 85% Reward: Teach Meditation to other leaders, reduce their stress levels, increase relations with other leaders.

    Roll: 92: Success

    Your fellows take to your advice like fish to water, and they all completely agree to your suggestion that your branch will remain secret. Officially, it’ll be so that CSG will be the only American Supernatural Organization. Unofficially, you’re going to be taking to the shadows in what is already supposed to be a shadow war. Focus on R and D will be on you, and they’ll make sure to inform you of anything large that is occurring, so long as you make sure that you do the same.

    To say they’re surprised by your spy network is putting it lightly. They hadn’t even known all of the Three Factions were so mobilized in the matters concerning Asia, let alone actually known that Asia was ruled entirely by vampires.

    Reward: All Leaders closer with one another, Reduced leader Stress, Leaders aware of your plans. Reduced Costs to Diplomatic Actions for next turn.

    A/N:

    Rumor Mill (Turn 3)

    Shattered Jade: The reason behind the stirring of the Ancient Jade empire has revealed itself: the Jing Dan, the Immortal Emperor has been killed by one of his daughters. The immense country is in the tumults of a massive civil war, with the minor factions that were once afraid now hovering around the country like crows. Vampires, always impressive in terms of magical ability, are fleeing China in droves and being taken in by the Three Factions. Your men have verified that the Devils and Angels are using some sort of system to turn creatures into their own kind, though the Angels seem to be more picky of whom they choose and they don’t seem to fully become angels... could it be God is gathering strength and thus relegating the task of expanding the Heavenly Host to the Angels themselves? Nevertheless, the perils of civil war provide unique opportunities… mainly to acquire some refugees and some ancient artifacts.

    Alliance Bound In Bloodstained Snow: The massive chimeric beasts of Russia have begun to travel in packs and roam their territories. The demi-human clans of Russia have allied with the human organization that killed the last Chimeric Beast, just a year ago. Though it is clear the trust is uneasy and both sides are greatly angered at one another, the humans are providing a clear advantage to the demi-human clans in providing mass-produced runic weaponry. The existence of such weapons are a surprise to all factions, whether minor or major, as the beast clans are quickly tearing into the massive creatures while armed with their new, powerful weaponry. The massive deposits located in the various territories are quickly being divided up in terms of wealth… it seems that the fear of the Cold War extending will ring true, as the influx of wealth has begun to grease the cogs of the Soviet Nation once more.

    Crossing Swords: The Templar, composed of all the minor human factions of Europe, have created the Caliburn Holy Sword Set and thus earned themselves another enemy. A small fleet of magical vessels have been assembled by the Church to take the Templar’s fortified stronghold, manned by Knights Hospitaller, a premier, professional army armed with weapons enchanted by light, and accompanied by two wielders of the Excalibur. The declaration of their intention is to demand the destruction of the Seven ‘Heretical’ Swords from the Templars, and to do such a thing themselves if the demands are not met. Though they have recently begun to receive supplies and modern weaponry from the modern world, many express doubts of their ability to survive the coming Armada of Faith while still beleaguered by their current opponents.

    Longinus Rising: The last sighting of the Sacred Gear used to kill Christ was over four hundred years ago, the spear being used to kill the last Lucifer before it and its user was killed in battle by the Devils. Found by a warlord in Africa, the wielder was of a tribe that the warlord sent his forces to attack. The third-world army, accompanied by several attack helicopters, was wiped out to the last man more than a kilometer away from the village. The spear, deadly against any foe and granting its wielder a shield that can shrug off most attacks, seems to still be in untrained hands given that it only struck from the current dimension instead of using its dimensional distortion effect to attack from multiple angles and different dimensions…

    Shock and Awe: Introducing itself to the world, the CSG of America has proven itself to be the source of the military technology advancement in a stunning, systematic sweep of all the major cities in America. They accrued many casualties and the mission was left uncompleted due to the fact many vagrants fled instead of risking battle, but the many victories were stunning and have drawn the attention of many downtrodden clans within America. As expected, the many minor races within America petitioned for aid in exchange for wealth and their offer was accepted. Perhaps for the first time since the country’s founding, Vagrants will no longer have free reign to come and do as they please without fear of reprisal. Still, many of the Major Factions note that the Vagrants will most likely band together and create perhaps the largest conglomeration of minor races to ever be seen, in order to counter the military-styled opponent they now face.

    Computers in Hell: Through the innovation of several prominent devils, computers are now able to be taken into hell without being fear of suddenly destroying themselves. The demand for the new device is skyrocketing…
